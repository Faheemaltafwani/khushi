My confession

My Bhoot,

I don't know how to begin something like this — because what I feel for you has never fit neatly into words. But here I am, trying anyway. Because you deserve more than silence. You deserve the truth, even when it's messy and incomplete.

I loved you — I still do — with every stubborn, imperfect, overwhelmingly devoted part of me. And somewhere in the middle of all that love, I made mistakes I wish I could undo. I controlled when I should have trusted. I demanded when I should have listened. I made you feel small when you were always the biggest thing in my world.

This is not an excuse. This is not a goodbye. This is me — just me, sitting with all of it, owning all of it, and asking for something I know I haven't always deserved: your forgiveness.

You were never just a relationship to me, Khushi. You were a promise I made to myself the moment I saw you.

Always yours, Faheem

My own words

The notes I wrote you

Khushi, sorry… really sorry. I myself don't understand why this keeps happening. You saw it yourself — I even told you not to create issues, and then suddenly it was like I completely changed. I'm really sorry for this.

I know this is not your fault at all. I never wanted anything in return from you, nor did I ask for anything. I may have threatened you emotionally sometimes, but I never actually did anything harmful. And I would never do anything to ruin your respect — even when your brother called my mother, I stayed respectful. Why? Because I genuinely cared for you.

Today I truly understood one thing: when a girl genuinely says she wants to leave, you shouldn't stop her — because emotionally she may have already left a long time ago. And my waiting was very precious to me. I learned from it too — like how one person can do absolutely anything to keep someone in their life, while the other person may not even realise it.

Khushi,

I know I was the one who made most of the mistakes. I accept that completely. I won't deny it, I won't hide from it. I know the things I did are not forgettable, and maybe not even forgivable. Still, I ask for your forgiveness — not because I deserve it, but because I owe you that honesty.

You also made mistakes. I know that. But today, I want you to know that I have forgiven all of them. Every single one. I carry no anger, no resentment. Just memories. And love.

I miss you, Khushi. I miss you like nothing else in my life. I still remember the first time we met — 7th May, 2022. I didn't plan it, I didn't expect it. But that was the first time I fell in love with someone so deeply, and so quickly. It scared me how fast it happened, but it also felt right.

This is not a message to bring you back, and it's not to make you feel guilty. It's just me being honest, maybe for the last time. You mattered to me more than I ever knew how to show.

I hope you are happy. Truly. And I hope someone gives you everything I couldn't.

Since the day you left, I feel like a void is growing inside me. The more I try to forget you, the more you come into my thoughts. I used to think I was strong and such things would never influence me — but seeing my condition, I know now how wrong I was.

I can literally hear you say my name in the morning telling me to go to class. But it ceases to be anything more than a dream when I open my eyes. Though we are away from each other, I can still feel your warmth.

I cherished every moment I spent with you. And if I had known you would leave — shayad main tumhare saath aur time bitata. But regrets won't change anything. What was going to happen already did. Milte hain kal — ab toh poori zindagi hai tumse baatein karne ko.

Three years ago, I met you — unexpectedly. You became so friendly with me though we hardly spoke before. In that cab — warna kisi ko mere hone ya na hone se koi farq hi nahi padti thi. But you chose me. And I couldn't have asked for anything better.

I know during these years, I used to dominate you, control you, and even made you cry. But my intentions were never to hurt you — it was my love and my care, misshapen by fear. I always thought we would never separate, so I committed myself completely — expected you to think the same. For you it may have been a normal relationship. For me it was not a relationship — it was a promise I made to you and to my soul.

And I will always keep this promise, although you don't believe my promises. But this one — this one you will see.
